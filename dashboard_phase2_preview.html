What's gone, what's reshuffled, what's promoted

  • Removed entirely: the standalone Data Sources panel. Replaced by the Readiness panel's "Source files" gate, which says the same thing ("4 of 5 sources") plus tells the FA whether the audit is uploaded AND confirmed. No info lost.
  • Removed entirely: the standalone FA Completion Checklist panel. The "4/7, 57% complete" is just a less-actionable version of Readiness's 5/9 with click-through actions. Readiness wins.
  • Demoted to a chip: PM Expense Review — "Sent May 11, Christina Bernard" inline next to the status pipeline. One look, no expand. Same info, fraction of the space.
  • Demoted to a counter: Pending Edits & Notes — kept because "41 items" is real signal Readiness doesn't track. Now a single amber bar saying "41 items pending review →". Click expands.
  • Promoted: Readiness panel becomes the centerpiece — fully expanded by default, all 9 gates visible with one-click actions. The FA sees the entire checklist at a glance.
  • Unchanged: header strip, status pipeline (now with PM chip), 4 KPI cards, workbook tabs below, wizard sidebar on the right.

Why this is the right move now (not 3 days ago)

  • The Readiness panel has matured into something the FA can actually act on. As of today, it has 9 gates (Source files, Audit confirmed, Period set, Building type, Orphan GLs, Duplicate rows, Payroll, Approved-file labels — new today, Generated). Each gate has a click-through action button. It IS the FA's to-do list.
  • The FA Completion Checklist was a parallel implementation when Readiness was less capable. Now they overlap completely.
  • Data Sources duplicates the audit-confirmed gate plus the new approved-file-labels gate.
  • Pending Edits is genuinely different (it tracks revision count, not setup completeness) so it earns its place as a single chip.

Code surface — what changes

  • The HTML for the 4 removed panels lives in workflow.py's BUILDING_DETAIL_TEMPLATE — straight deletion + a couple of style adjustments to promote Readiness.
  • The Readiness panel's existing collapse/expand logic flips: it's expanded by default; the "all gates green" auto-collapse from the May 9 implementation still kicks in when there's nothing to do.
  • The "Pending Edits & Notes" data still flows from /api/dashboard/<ec>.audit_summary and the revision count; just the rendering shrinks.
  • The PM Expense Review's "Sent May 11" data comes from Budget.pm_sent_at — already a single field; just render it inline in the pipeline header.
  • Net diff: roughly -200 lines of HTML + ~50 lines reshuffled. Low-risk because no API changes.
