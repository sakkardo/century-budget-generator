Commercial Rent + Escalations

Design preview for integrating the Excel Comm Rent & Escalations sheet into the product. Below: what the feature would look like, how data flows, and three integration options ranked by scope.

1

Portfolio coverage — what we found

Scanned all 143 buildings' 2026 approved Excel files. 20 have commercial rent / escalation rows. Four distinct patterns:

~7
Multi-tenant + RE Tax escalation
e.g. 148: 3 tenants with multi-year rent schedules; RE Tax pass-through math (total tax × applied rate × tenant share)
~5
Single tenant + utility/insurance billback
e.g. 212: one garage tenant; tenant pays share of (actual − base year) on Gas, Electric, Steam, Insurance
~3
Multi-tenant + OpEx escalation
e.g. 829: modified-gross lease — tenant pays share of total operating expenses minus base year
~5
Other (common charges, no commercial)
e.g. 842 (Citarella in Apt 8J), 869 (condo) — commercial flows through different rows, not this sheet

All 20 buildings feed into the same Summary rows: 4040-0000 Commercial Rent and 4520-0000 Commercial RE Tax Escalation. Some also use 4250-0000 Utility Income and 4515-0010 Property Insurance billbacks. Today the system aggregates these into a single Summary number with zero per-tenant detail. The Excel sheet stays the source of truth, invisible to the product.

The Excel formulas map cleanly to the product's existing data model. No new GL infrastructure needed.

esc_portion = total − base_year

One-time importer parses the existing Excel sheet on first open, populates the new tables, and the FA confirms. After that, the system is the source of truth and exports back to Excel format on demand.

4

Three integration options — pick scope

A · Viewer only

Effort: 1–2 days CC · Completeness: 4/10

Parse the Excel Comm Rent & Escalations sheet on dashboard load. Display tenant cards read-only inside a new tab. No editing. Excel remains the source of truth.

Pros

  • Smallest lift, fastest ship
  • FA sees lease detail without opening Excel
  • Zero risk of data divergence

Cons

  • FA still maintains Excel as source — no time savings
  • No write-back to Summary — duplicates the current pain

B · Edit + auto-feed Summary Recommended

Effort: 1–2 weeks CC · Completeness: 8/10

New Commercial tab with editable tenant cards + escalation engine. Auto-feeds Summary rows (4040, 4520, etc.). One-time importer pulls from existing Excel. System becomes the source of truth.

Pros

  • Replaces ~50% of the time spent in the commercial rent Excel sheet
  • Tenant detail finally visible to the system (notes, lease expiry alerts, etc.)
  • Eliminates the "Why is Commercial Rent showing $432K?" inspection trip
  • Fits the existing pattern (like Payroll / Energy tabs)
  • Handles all 3 escalation models with one UI

Cons

  • Some buildings (842, 869) need separate handling — different commercial models
  • Edge cases on lease expiry mid-year

C · Full lease management

Effort: 1–2 months CC · Completeness: 10/10

B + multi-year scheduling, lease expiry alerts (drawer integration), tenant payment tracking, full audit trail, export back to legacy Excel format. Excel disappears entirely for commercial rent.

Pros

  • FA never touches Excel for commercial rent
  • Auto-alerts on upcoming lease expirations (huge for Mack Dermatology cases)
  • Full historical visibility

Cons

  • Scope creep risk — lease management is its own product
  • Audit / legal review may be needed before going source-of-truth
  • Tenant payment tracking is out of scope of the budget app

Recommendation: ship Option B in two phases.

Phase 1 (3–4 days): read-only viewer (Option A) for the 20 buildings with the sheet. Validates the data model + UI without write risk.
Phase 2 (1 week): edit + auto-feed to Summary for the 3 patterns we've identified. Skip the condo/Citarella cases initially — handle in a follow-up.
After Phase 2 the FA can stop touching the Excel sheet for the 15 buildings that fit the patterns cleanly. The other 5 stay Excel-based for now.

5

Open questions for you

  1. Source of truth flip. If the system becomes authoritative, do we need to also produce an Excel export so legal/audit reviews stay on the same format?
  2. Base year handling. Base year tax/opex is hardcoded per tenant in current Excels. Should this be editable by FA, or admin-only?
  3. Lease document storage. Excel has plain-text comments about lease terms. Should the new feature also store / link to the actual lease PDFs?
  4. Mid-year lease changes. When a new lease is signed mid-year (Mack Dermatology pattern), the rent table needs a "from this date" rollover. Worth special UI?
  5. Tenant share calculation. Most leases hardcode the share %. Some pull from a building "Tenant Schedule" doc. Source for share %?
  6. Building 842 (Citarella in Apt 8J) + 869 (condo) patterns. These don't fit the "Comm Rent & Escalations sheet" pattern. Handle in this scope or defer?
