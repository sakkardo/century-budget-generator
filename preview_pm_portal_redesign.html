DESIGN MOVES
1Locked context recedes
Prior Year / YTD / Accrual / Unpaid go warm-gray on warm-bg. Reads but doesn't shout. Smaller weight reinforces "this is history, not action."
2Current Budget is the anchor
Bumped out of the recessive locked group with bolder text, warmer background, and a right-edge divider. Sits as the visual bookend before the action zone — it's the baseline every PM decision is compared against.
3Action zone framed in brown
Increase % + Increase $ + No Change get a dark-brown header band and gold side rails. Three columns visually become one chunk: "Your decision."
4Skip button is ghost until clicked
Previously mint-filled (competes with success state). Now dashed-border ghost. Clicking flips it to a blue confirmed chip — distinct from green "you entered a value" success.
5"Why did this change" → info dot
Was the loudest element on screen (yellow pill). Now a quiet amber (i) circle with tooltip. Same information, no longer outshouting the action.
6Row-state left stripe
Three-pixel color rail tells the PM at a glance: green = you acted, blue = you skipped, amber = system flagged, none = untouched. Scrollable progress without reading every cell.
7Variance bold, % Δ muted
$ Variance becomes the primary diagnostic (bold, full-color red/green). % Change demotes to a quieter secondary indicator. One dominant variance signal, not two competing.
8Grouping band above headers
CONTEXT & HISTORY · YOUR DECISION · RESULT · NOTES. Five seconds of orientation for a new PM. Scannable on any building.
9Negatives use real minus sign
"−$2,488" instead of "$-2,488". Same number of pixels, professional finance convention, easier to scan in tabular columns.
